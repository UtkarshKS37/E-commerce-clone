India win the ICC T20 World Cup 2024

The drought of ICC tournament victories is over for India. India valiantly fought and clenched onto their dreams of winning the world cup and accomplished the task after a long 13 years as they go undefeated throughout the torunament. India win the World Cup in the T20 format after 17 years in a nail-biting, close match-up thriller by defying all odds and predictions, and defeating the alleged "Choker Champions" South Africa.

CONTRIBUTIONS FOR THE VICTORY

As captain, Rohit Sharma led the team with strategic acumen and scored crucial runs at the top of the order, ensuring solid starts in multiple games. His leadership was instrumental in navigating the team through challenging phases and finally fulfilled his destiny by winning the T20 World Cup, and then retired from the format.

Virat Kohli was the backbone of India's batting, scoring a critical 76 in the final. Although his batting looked lacklustre during the tournament, but in the final his ability to anchor the innings were key to India's success, saving the Indian batting from yet another collapse when it mattered the most. He announced his retirement from T20Is after winning the T20 World Cup, having completed white ball cricket, leaving on a high note.​

Jasprit Bumrah lead the bowling attack with 15 wickets, choking the run rate and providing a breakthrough whenever required, at an exceptional economy below 5 in the T20 World Cup. His spell in the final was decisive, earning him the Player of the Tournament award. He, with his ability to bowl multiple variations along with his unique action, is truly the best bowler of this generation regardless of the format.

Returning from injury, Pant provided aggressive middle-order batting, with his notable 42 and crucial catches against Pakistan setting the tone in the group stage. His sharp wicketkeeping was vital in crucial moments of the matches.

Suryakumar Yadav was India's second-highest run-scorer, adapting his play according to the situation. His 47 in the semi-final against England, sharp fielding and undoubtedly the best catch of this career in the final were the highlights of his campaign.

Shivam Dube contributed with vital late-order runs, including a quick 27 in the final, helping India reach a competitive total. His role as a finisher was crucial in tight matches​. As an all-rounder, he was also an extra bowling option for the captain.

Axar excelled both with bat and ball and in the field, playing a pivotal role as a floater in the batting order and delivering key spells with his left-arm spin. His marvellous 47 in the final and tight-ended bowling spells, like against England were crucial for India's defense​ He stood out in the team and justified the title of "Bapu" given to him.

Hardik Pandya's all-round performances were invaluable, including a match-winning 50 against Bangladesh and crucial overs in the final. His ability to score quickly and bowl under pressure effectively made him a standout player and rightfully earn the #1 rank in the ICC T20I all-rounders rankings. He went from being booed and trolled during the IPL to becoming a national hero.

Despite a below-par tournament, Ravindra Jadeja's contributions included a crucial 17 in the semi-final. His fielding remained top-notch, although his bowling was not as effective as expected. Nevertheless, he ended his T20I career as champion.

Kuldeep Yadav's inclusion in the Super Eights strengthened India's spin attack. His performances against Australia and England, where he took crucial wickets, along with good fielding were instrumental in India's progress to the final.

Arshdeep Singh was India's leading wicket-taker, providing crucial breakthroughs and maintaining pressure on the opposition with his accurate bowling. His performances in the final stages were particularly noteworthy.

Although Siraj's tournament was cut short after the group stage, his initial spells helped set the tone for the bowling unit. His ability to swing the ball and bowl economically was a valuable asset, and could have been beneficiary in the finals where spin bowling was being wrecked​.

Coach Rahul Dravid left the helms of the team pecefully, winning the T20 World Cup on his last day as coach of the team, having earned the title of champion which evaded him in his playing career. India had Yashasvi Jaiswal, Sanju Samson and Yuzvendra Chahal on the bench who also became champions.
